## Al-Khawarizmi Online School of Computing

In honour of Muhammad ibn Musa al-Khwarizmi and his Kitāb al-Jabr wa-l-Muqābala — one of the birthplaces of organised thought about algebra, algorithms, and by extension modern computing — and especially in honour of its beautiful, clear prose and the pronouncedly non-secular nature of its worldview, I would like to share some tutorials written in the spirit of his work.

This spirit, in my opinion, embodies the true nature of technology: an instrument for the mitigation of human hardship and the establishment of greater fairness in society. The more people are equipped with these tools, the more just and prosperous society becomes.

One who wishes to seek this knowledge should be greeted with open arms and guided in small steps through the landscape of various problems. Once the problems are understood, he is then taught to match each problem with its recommended solution, and grows calmer and more confident the more he learns.

He then proceeds to explore the minutiae of the proposed solutions so that he may combine them, adapt them, or even create entirely new ones. Much like a doctor, he learns to observe problems, diagnose them carefully, apply suitable remedies or combinations of remedies, and devise new solutions where necessary.

The key is to look at methods, tools, algorithms, languages, and technologies not as isolated things in themselves, nor as masters, but as servants of the doctors — instruments placed in the hands of those who seek to understand and remedy the problems of the world.
